# Fusion Energy Engineering — Podcast Episode Candidates

Curated for a technically sophisticated listener who wants fusion **reactor architecture**
depth (confinement physics, magnets, materials, plant design) rather than "fusion is 30
years away" news coverage. Twelve candidates below span all six target confinement
approaches: tokamak, stellarator, inertial confinement (laser and pulsed-power driven),
field-reversed configuration, Z-pinch, and magnetized target fusion.

Every episode was matched against Apple's iTunes Search/Lookup API
(`itunes.apple.com/search` and `itunes.apple.com/lookup`) to pull canonical
`collectionId` / `trackId` / `episodeGuid` / `trackViewUrl` data, and each episode's show
notes or a description of them was independently fetched to confirm subject matter before
inclusion. No IDs or URLs below were invented — where a field could not be verified it is
`null` and flagged in the caveats. A few strong leads turned up in research (a Type One
Energy‑specific podcast interview, a dedicated recent Titans of Nuclear episode on
Commonwealth Fusion/SPARC or on General Fusion) could not be located as verifiable podcast
episodes via the iTunes API within the search effort spent, so they are omitted rather than
guessed at — see the note at the end.

## 1. Lex Fridman Podcast — "#353 – Dennis Whyte: Nuclear Fusion and the Future of Energy"

- **Release date:** 2023-01-21
- **Duration:** ~194 minutes (11,617,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 1434243584 · **trackId:** 1000595885259
- **episodeGuid:** `https://lexfridman.com/?p=5364`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/353-dennis-whyte-nuclear-fusion-and-the-future-of-energy/id1434243584?i=1000595885259
- **feedUrl:** https://lexfridman.com/feed/podcast/
- **Episode page:** https://lexfridman.com/dennis-whyte/

Dennis Whyte directs MIT's Plasma Science and Fusion Center and co-founded Commonwealth
Fusion Systems. Over more than three hours he goes deep on tokamak physics — magnetic vs.
inertial confinement, the Lawson criterion, plasma instabilities, high-temperature
superconducting (HTS) magnets, and how SPARC/ARC's REBCO tape magnets differ from ITER's
niobium-tin magnets. Substantial ITER and SPARC comparison, plus engineering trade-offs
(tritium breeding, first-wall materials, neutron damage).

- **Reactor types:** Tokamak, ITER, SPARC / Commonwealth Fusion Systems
- **Depth:** High
- **Caveats:** Long-form (3h+), wide-ranging (also covers policy/history); a technical
  listener can skip the first hour of introductory material if desired.

## 2. Lex Fridman Podcast — "#485 – David Kirtley: Nuclear Fusion, Plasma Physics, and the Future of Energy"

- **Release date:** 2025-11-17
- **Duration:** ~165 minutes (9,922,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 1434243584 · **trackId:** 1000737133630
- **episodeGuid:** `https://lexfridman.com/?p=6354`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/485-david-kirtley-nuclear-fusion-plasma-physics-and/id1434243584?i=1000737133630
- **feedUrl:** https://lexfridman.com/feed/podcast/
- **Episode page:** null (not independently located; lexfridman.com episode-page slug not confirmed beyond the Apple link)

David Kirtley is CEO of Helion Energy. Covers Helion's pulsed field-reversed-configuration
(FRC) approach: colliding and compressing two FRC plasmoids magnetically, direct energy
recovery via induction (no steam turbine), the fusion-fuel cycle (D-³He), and why Helion
targets a 2028 commercial plant. Contrasts FRC pulsed operation against steady-state
tokamak/stellarator designs.

- **Reactor types:** Field-reversed configuration (FRC), Helion
- **Depth:** High
- **Caveats:** Very recent (Nov 2025); CEO interview so somewhat promotional in tone, but
  Kirtley goes deep on plasma physics and direct-energy-conversion engineering.

## 4. Titans of Nuclear — "Ben Levitt, Zap Energy"

- **Release date:** 2021-06-21
- **Duration:** ~79 minutes (4,726,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 1331598443 · **trackId:** 1000526273477
- **episodeGuid:** `f2f7185f-0822-4209-9fc8-898558811f39`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/ben-levitt-zap-energy/id1331598443?i=1000526273477
- **feedUrl:** https://rss.libsyn.com/shows/107828/destinations/585143.xml

Ben Levitt (Zap Energy co-founder/CSO) traces the Z-pinch concept from 18th-century
observations of electrical discharges through to Zap's sheared-flow-stabilized Z-pinch —
which avoids the large superconducting magnets and cryogenics tokamaks/stellarators need by
using the plasma's own current to self-confine, stabilized by sheared axial flow. Covers
what "scientific and commercial breakeven" mean in this architecture.

- **Reactor types:** Z-pinch (sheared-flow-stabilized), Zap Energy
- **Depth:** Medium-High
- **Caveats:** Oldest pick in the set (2021) — pre-dates Zap's more recent FuZE-Q and
  liquid-metal-wall results, so treat company milestones as dated even though the core
  physics explanation holds up. No newer Zap-specific long-form interview could be
  verified via the iTunes API in this search.

- **Release date:** 2023-01-18
- **Duration:** ~54.5 minutes (3,269,000 ms)

Recorded right after LLNL's December 2022 ignition announcement. Tammy Ma (who leads LLNL's
Inertial Fusion Energy institutional initiative) explains NIF's 192-beam laser system, the
hohlraum/capsule target design, and what "fusion reactions, lasers, and the various
components involved" in ignition actually mean, plus what it takes to move from a
once-a-day laser shot to a repetition-rate power plant.

- **Reactor types:** Inertial confinement fusion (laser-driven), NIF / Lawrence Livermore
- **Depth:** High
- **Caveats:** None significant. LLNL's own "Big Ideas Lab" podcast has newer, shorter
  (~15-18 min) episodes on IFE engineering specifics if supplementary material is wanted:
  "Inertial Fusion Energy" (trackId 1000698723356, Mar 2025), "Discovery Science at NIF"
  (trackId 1000749072865, Feb 2026), and "Target Fabrication" (trackId 1000754445596,
  Mar 2026) — collectionId 1766302914, feedUrl https://feeds.simplecast.com/WfThhbae.

## 7. omega tau — "The Wendelstein 7-X Fusion Experiment" (Episode 312)

- **Release date:** 2019-05-11
- **Duration:** ~208 minutes / 3h28m (12,470,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 350159142 ("omega tau — English only") · **trackId:** 1000437810265
- **episodeGuid:** `http://omegataupodcast.net/?p=4577`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-wendelstein-7-x-fusion-experiment/id350159142?i=1000437810265
- **feedUrl:** https://omegataupodcast.net/show/en/feed/mp3/

Host Markus Voelter tours the Wendelstein 7-X hall at the Max Planck Institute for Plasma
Physics with experimentalist Matthias Hirsch, then talks theory/modeling with Ralf Kleiber.
Covers tokamak-vs-stellarator trade-offs, the twisted non-planar coil geometry that gives
W7-X its "quasi-isodynamic" optimized field, magnetohydrodynamics, gyrokinetic simulation,
and the engineering of the superconducting coil set itself.

- **Reactor types:** Stellarator, Wendelstein 7-X
- **Depth:** High
- **Caveats:** Older (2019) but still the most technically detailed English-language
  stellarator-engineering podcast episode found; omega tau's back catalogue (ended
  publishing new episodes in 2023) remains available on all major platforms. Show notes
  suggest prior fusion episodes (22, 157, 304) as useful background.

## 8. omega tau — "Superconductivity" (Episode 285)

- **Release date:** 2018-06-08
- **Duration:** ~171 minutes / 2h51m (10,250,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 350159142 · **trackId:** 1000413296110
- **episodeGuid:** `http://omegataupodcast.net/?p=4062`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/superconductivity/id350159142?i=1000413296110
- **feedUrl:** https://omegataupodcast.net/show/en/feed/mp3/

Guest Pierre Bauer is a superconducting-magnet engineer at ITER. Timestamped segments cover
superconductivity basics, applications (ITER, MRI, LHC), materials (niobium-tin, niobium
titanium), magnet design, and quench protection — i.e., the magnet engineering that
actually makes a tokamak's confining field possible. Not a "reactor tour" episode, but the
single most magnet-engineering-dense episode found across all shows searched.

- **Reactor types:** Tokamak (magnet engineering), ITER
- **Depth:** High
- **Caveats:** Framed around superconductivity broadly, not fusion specifically — roughly
  half the runtime is general applications (MRI, particle accelerators) before and around
  the ITER material. Include as a magnet-engineering deep-dive companion to the Whyte and
  W7-X episodes, not a standalone "what is a tokamak" primer.

## 9. CleanTechies Podcast — "#216 This is How Nuclear Fusion Becomes a Reality w/ Brian Berzin (Thea Energy)"

- **Release date:** 2024-10-24
- **Duration:** ~32 minutes (1,919,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 1555107027 · **trackId:** 1000723533213
- **episodeGuid:** `substack:post:150519704`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/216-this-is-how-nuclear-fusion-becomes-a-reality-w/id1555107027?i=1000723533213
- **feedUrl:** https://api.substack.com/feed/podcast/1541903.rss

Brian Berzin (Thea Energy co-founder/CEO, electrical engineer) explains Thea's core
architectural bet: replacing a stellarator's handful of exotically-shaped, precision-milled
superconducting coils with an array of ~350 simple planar coils, whose combined field is
shaped in software rather than hardware. Directly contrasts this against the W7-X approach
of hyper-precise non-planar coils.

- **Reactor types:** Stellarator (planar-coil architecture), Thea Energy
- **Depth:** Medium-High
- **Caveats:** Shorter, founder-interview format (not a deep-dive show), so covers the
  planar-coil concept clearly but doesn't go as deep on plasma physics fundamentals as the
  W7-X or Whyte episodes. Good as the "how do you make a stellarator manufacturable"
  companion to Episode 7 above.

## 10. Catalyst with Shayle Kann — "A blueprint for scalable fusion power"

- **Release date:** 2026-05-21
- **Duration:** ~40.6 minutes (2,435 sec)
- **Apple collectionId:** 1593204897 · **trackId:** 1000768899584
- **episodeGuid:** `540b378e-5489-11f1-af4d-3398e7577d2e`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/a-blueprint-for-scalable-fusion-power/id1593204897?i=1000768899584
- **feedUrl:** https://feeds.megaphone.fm/catalyst

Carrie von Muench, COO of Pacific Fusion, discusses the company's pulsed-power approach to
inertial confinement fusion: an impedance-matched Marx generator (IMG) driver architecture
— many modular, shipping-container-sized pulser units firing in tight synchrony to
implode a target — as a manufacturable alternative to NIF's laser driver. Covers net
facility gain vs. net plant gain, steady-state vs. pulsed trade-offs, and modular-plant
strategy for scaling a fusion "power plant" rather than a physics experiment.

- **Reactor types:** Inertial confinement fusion (pulsed-power/Marx-generator driver),
  Pacific Fusion
- **Depth:** Medium-High
- **Caveats:** Very recent (May 2026); useful as a contrast case to NIF's laser-driven ICF
  approach (different driver, same target-implosion physics). Investor/business framing
  from a COO, so plant-economics content is mixed in with the architecture discussion.

## 11. Lab to Market Leadership (with Chris Reichhelm) — "Nuclear Fusion Breakthrough: The Tokamak Energy Story | Dr. David Kingham"

- **Release date:** 2025-06-03
- **Duration:** ~62 minutes (3,722,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 1745548847 · **trackId:** 1000710979894
- **episodeGuid:** `Buzzsprout-17267626`
- **Apple Podcasts URL:** https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/lab-to-market-leadership-with-chris-reichhelm/id1745548847?i=1000710979894
- **feedUrl:** https://rss.buzzsprout.com/2317730.rss

Dr. David Kingham co-founded Tokamak Energy. Traces the company from a three-person startup
to its high-field, compact **spherical tokamak** program (ST40), covering the
high-temperature superconducting (HTS) magnet technology central to Tokamak Energy's bet —
smaller, cheaper, higher-field magnets enabling a compact spherical tokamak instead of a
large conventional one like ITER — and the shift toward licensing magnet/HTS IP alongside
building reactors.

- **Reactor types:** Spherical tokamak, Tokamak Energy, ST40, HTS magnets
- **Depth:** Medium-High
- **Caveats:** Business/leadership-podcast framing (host focuses partly on
  entrepreneurship lessons), so plasma-physics depth is lighter than the Whyte or omega tau
  episodes, but it is the most substantive verifiable Tokamak Energy engineering interview
  located.

- **Release date:** 2026-04-10
- **Duration:** ~54 minutes (3,248,000 ms)
- **Apple collectionId:** 151485663 · **trackId:** 1000760722598
- **episodeGuid:** `ideas-01af4f0e-aca2-4775-962a-8f1e779c809d`

CEO). Covers General Fusion's magnetized target fusion (MTF) architecture — injecting a
compact toroid of plasma into a liquid-metal-lined vessel and compressing it with
mechanically driven pistons — plus broader energy-security and climate framing.

- **Reactor types:** Magnetized target fusion (MTF), General Fusion
- **Depth:** Medium
- **Caveats:** This is the weakest-depth pick in the set: it's a CBC current-affairs radio
  documentary, not an engineering-focused interview, and General Fusion's MTF architecture
  is one of several threads (geopolitics/climate framing takes real airtime). No dedicated,
  verifiable long-form technical MTF/General-Fusion podcast interview could be located via
  the iTunes API in this search — this is included because MTF coverage was otherwise
  missing entirely from the list, not because it's a top-tier technical episode. If a
  stronger General Fusion or Type One Energy episode is found later, consider swapping it
